# Cybersecurity Training & Practice

> [!info] The Path to Mastery
> Nobody is born a cybersecurity expert. Mastery requires continuous practice and training. Crucially, this requires safe, isolated [[Lab Environments]] to ensure practice doesn't harm live production systems.

## Organizational Value
* **Proactive Defense**: "An ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure." Training reduces the likelihood of incidents and prevents the panic of learning new concepts during a live cyber attack.
* **Capacity Building**: Increases a team's effective capacity and readiness without requiring new hires.
* **Efficiency**: Provides a fast ramp-up for junior staff while saving senior staff from repeatedly teaching the same foundational material.

## Standardization & Team Dynamics
* **Common Baseline**: Centralized training establishes clear, assessable skill metrics, replacing vague titles like "junior" or "senior."
* **Actionable Insights**: Clear visibility into current skills helps leadership make informed decisions for task assignment and defines clear career progression paths for employees.
* **Team Building**: Training can be highly engaging. Participating in group exercises like [[CTF]] (Capture the Flag) challenges fosters teamwork and builds strong relationships.

# Customizing Security Training

> [!info] Scaling Training Needs
> The approach to cybersecurity training must adapt as an organization grows or its requirements become more specialized.

## Team Size & Customization
* **Small Teams**: Off-the-shelf, standardized training is usually the most logical and efficient choice.
* **Large Teams (>20 employees) or Specific Needs**: Customizing existing training becomes essential to increase efficacy.
	* *Example*: Utilizing platforms like **[[TryHackMe]]**'s "Content Studio" to modify or create bespoke modules tailored directly to an organization's specific priorities and threats.

## Enterprise Integration
> [!note] 
> Large corporations require seamlessly integrated solutions rather than isolated, standalone platforms.

* **Key Requirements for Enterprise**:
	* **[[SSO]] (Single Sign-On)**: For unified, streamlined, and secure user access.
	* **Well-documented [[API|APIs]]**: To ensure the training platform can communicate and integrate smoothly with the organization's existing software landscape.
